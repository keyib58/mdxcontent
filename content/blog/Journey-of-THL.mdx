<div style={{ margin: "40px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
  <h3 className="text-lg font-bold">What is RTP and Why Does It Matter?</h3>
  <p>
    Return to Player (RTP) is a percentage that indicates the theoretical payout
    a slot game provides over an extended period. For instance, a game with a
    97% RTP returns $97 for every $100 wagered in the long run. While RTP does
    not determine individual session outcomes, it influences overall player
    perception and trust in a game’s fairness.
  </p>
  <p style={{ margin: "10px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
    From an operator’s perspective, RTP plays a vital role in balancing
    profitability and player retention. While lower RTPs may enhance margins,
    excessively reducing them can deter players. The ideal approach involves
    offering varied RTP options – through a diverse library of games – while
    maintaining transparency and fair play.
  </p>
</div>

<div style={{ margin: "20px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
  <h3 className="text-lg font-bold">Slot Volatility: Risk vs Reward</h3>
  <p>
    Volatility measures the frequency and size of payouts in a slot game. It
    categorises slots into three main types:
  </p>
  <p style={{ margin: "10px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
    Low Volatility: Frequent but smaller wins, ideal for casual players seeking
    extended playtime.
  </p>
  <p style={{ margin: "10px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
    Medium Volatility: A balanced mix of payout frequency and size, appealing to
    a broad audience.
  </p>
  <p style={{ margin: "10px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
    High Volatility: Larger but less frequent payouts, attracting thrill-seekers
    chasing big wins.
  </p>
  <p style={{ margin: "10px auto 0px", textAlign: "justify" }}>
    A well-balanced slot portfolio should cater to different player segments.
    High-volatility games drive excitement and engagement, while low-volatility
    titles provide steady entertainment. This variety ensures player retention
    and sustained revenue streams for operators.
  </p>
</div>
